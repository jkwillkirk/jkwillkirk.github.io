Favorite Project

I was assigned to upgrade old automatic cable testing machines running Windows seven too slowly. Due to the requirements of the communication card needing a particular slot on the motherboard that is absent on modern motherboards, the only computer I found with the correct specification was in the design office. I upgraded the design office to use the old workstations for automatic cable testing machines to enable me to utilise the workstations. Once doing this, another challenge presented itself; the standard configuration of Windows 10 resulted in the communication speed being too fast for the old machinery. This resulted In me having to tweak, test, and re-tweak the windows 10 configuration settings, eventually settling on four cores out of the eight. To compensate for the slow processor, I left the 32GB of Random Access Memory on the PC. The solution I have implemented is much quicker, almost as fast as the new machines.
