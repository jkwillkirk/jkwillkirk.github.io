Strategic Interview Briefing: RPA Developer

Derbyshire Constabulary Panel: T. Litowczuk & S. Adkin

Deconstructing the Strategy: Our Process

This section provides an interactive breakdown of the analytical journey we undertook. It details how the initial intelligence was synthesized to produce the final, targeted interview preparation strategy.

What: We began with a collection of documents: the Job Description, organizational context from the careers website, the formal interview invitation, and our email correspondence regarding reasonable adjustments.

Why: The initial analysis revealed a critical contradiction. The Job Description detailed a highly technical, executional developer role. However, your past interview experience with another force involved very high-level, strategic questions. This created ambiguity about the true nature of the upcoming interview.

The Problem: We needed to resolve this conflict to create an accurate preparation strategy. Relying on the wrong assumption would lead to preparing for the wrong interview.

What: A critical piece of intelligence was located: the "Interview Preparation Guidance" PDF linked in the invitation. This document explicitly stated the interview would be competency-based and referenced the College of Policing's Competency and Values Framework (CVF).

Why: This was the breakthrough. It provided the formal assessment framework the interviewers would be using. It superseded any assumptions based on past experience and gave us a clear, evidence-based direction for our analysis.

The Shift: Our focus shifted from broad speculation to a targeted analysis of how the specific competencies in the CVF would be applied to test for the skills listed in the RPA Developer job description.

Who: Further intelligence gathering provided detailed professional profiles for the two interviewers: Taras Litowczuk and Sharon Adkin.

Why: This was the final layer of critical detail. The analysis revealed they were not a homogenous panel but represented a duality of interests:

  • Taras Litowczuk: The Technical Architect. Focused on technical excellence, security, architecture, and the Microsoft Power Platform. He represents the "how."
  • Sharon Adkin: The Business Strategist. Focused on business value, ROI, resource management, and filling the "skills gap." She represents the "why."

The Insight: We concluded the interview would be a test of our ability to satisfy both stakeholders simultaneously. This was not a generic competency interview; it was a highly specific assessment against two distinct but complementary sets of priorities.

What: All prior analysis was synthesized into the final, multi-layered interactive tool you are now using.

How: The generic question framework was discarded and rewritten. The application was re-architected to reflect the true nature of the interview. The structure was designed to allow you to prepare for each interviewer's likely questions and core competencies separately.

The Outcome: The result is a highly targeted, evidence-based preparation tool. It moves beyond generic advice and provides a clear, actionable framework for navigating the specific strategic landscape of this interview, addressing the known priorities of the key decision-makers.

Intelligence Analysis of the Interview Landscape

This section provides a comprehensive strategic analysis of the upcoming interview. Use this intelligence to understand the context, the key stakeholders, and the underlying objectives of the assessment process.

The interview is a multi-faceted evaluation of your ability to operate at the intersection of deep technical implementation and pragmatic business strategy. The panel, comprising Taras Litowczuk and Sharon Adkin, represents a critical duality. You must demonstrate fluency in the languages of both the technical architect and the business stakeholder.

The core dynamic is a synthesis of two priorities: Taras Litowczuk, as Lead Developer, embodies the technical vision (how to innovate securely), while Sharon Adkin, as Business Lead, represents pragmatic business value (why the organization should innovate).

This role was created to fill a recognized "skills gap" and internalize automation capabilities. You must position yourself as a comprehensive solution provider who can build robust, secure solutions that satisfy technical standards, while also delivering quantifiable efficiency gains and operational value that align with the public sector mission.

A. Professional Trajectory: From User Support to Digital Innovator

His career path from front-line technical support to Lead Developer provides him with an intimate, first-hand knowledge of the user pain points he now seeks to eliminate. His focus on "user-centered" solutions is a core principle forged through direct experience. Frame your technical examples through a user-centric lens, articulating how stakeholder engagement and intuitive design solved a tangible problem for the end-user.

B. The Technical Ecosystem: The Microsoft Powerhouse

The environment is centered on the Microsoft stack. He is a Subject Matter Expert in Azure, Dynamics 365, and the Power Platform, with a 90% proficiency in Power Automate. This is the single most significant technical data point. The interview will be a test of specialized, platform-specific knowledge. Deep, demonstrable expertise in Power Automate (cloud and desktop flows), Dataverse, SharePoint, and supporting Azure services is paramount.

C. Leadership Philosophy: Driving Transformation

He leads a cross-functional team and is maturing the development practice by establishing best practices, coding standards, and Application Lifecycle Management (ALM). His philosophy is built on a foundation of security, emphasizing a "data-first model." You must present yourself as a professional developer, not just a coder. This involves discussing source control (Git), deployment pipelines (CI/CD), clean code, robust error handling, secure credential management (Azure Key Vault), and comprehensive logging.

A. Role Analysis: Bridge Between Business Need and IT Solution

Her progression from Project Manager to Business Lead signifies a shift from tactical delivery to strategic alignment. She is the voice of the "internal customer" and the steward of public funds. Any technical discussion must be framed in the language of business value. Be prepared to answer the "so what?" question for every technical capability. Structure your answers as concise business cases.

B. Key Priorities: Value, Resources, and Capability Gaps

A key data point is her focus on using contractors to fill an "internal skills gap" while "locking in the day rate." The decision to hire a permanent developer is the strategic evolution of this process. This role exists to provide a better long-term investment than external contractors. You must position yourself as the strategic solution: a long-term asset who can build a sustainable, in-house automation capability, create reusable components, and contribute to a center of excellence model.

Every technology initiative must serve the core mission of "reducing and investigating crime, protecting people and promoting law and order." The Constabulary's values emphasize a "people first" culture and a robust Health and Wellbeing Strategy.

In this context, automation must be framed as a mechanism for empowerment, not workforce reduction. The primary goal is to free up highly trained officers and staff from repetitive administrative tasks, allowing them to focus on core policing duties. Your ability to connect your work directly to key performance metrics like "Preventing crime," "Protecting vulnerable people," and "Good use of resources" will be immensely powerful.

Assessing the Technical Architect: Taras Litowczuk

These questions test your technical depth, architectural mindset, and alignment with a user-centered, security-first development philosophy within the Microsoft ecosystem. The goal is to prove you are a professional developer who can build secure, scalable, and maintainable solutions.

  • Describe your most complex automation built using Power Automate, detailing your use of both cloud flows and desktop flows.
  • Explain how you have used Dataverse as a secure and scalable backend for a Power App or automation.
  • Tell us about a time you built a solution that deeply integrated Power Automate and SharePoint Online for document management.
  • Describe a complex problem you solved using PowerShell. How did it interact with other M365 or Azure services?
  • How do you decide when to use a Power Automate cloud flow versus a desktop flow? What are the architectural trade-offs?
  • What is your experience with Power Platform ALM and using solutions to move components between environments? Why is this critical?
  • How have you used or would you use AI Builder to create more intelligent automations?
  • What is your experience with version control systems like Azure DevOps or Git in the context of Power Platform development?
  • How would you approach debugging a complex cloud flow that is failing intermittently, using the tools available within the Power Platform?
  • What area of the Power Platform or Azure are you currently seeking to develop, and what steps are you taking to do so?
  • How would you design an audit trail for a solution that updates records in Dataverse for legal defensibility?
  • Describe your experience implementing security roles in Dataverse to enforce role-based access control.
  • What is your preferred method for securing credentials and API keys for a Power Automate flow, referencing Azure Key Vault?
  • Imagine you discover an existing cloud flow that stores a password in plain text. What are your immediate actions?
  • How do you ensure security is maintained throughout the entire Power Platform ALM?
  • Explain the role of Azure Active Directory and service principals in authentication for a solution using a custom connector.
  • Describe your experience deploying a Power Platform solution into a 24/7 live environment. How did you use solutions management to minimize disruption?
  • What is your strategy for testing a Power Automate desktop flow to ensure it is robust enough for a mission-critical environment?
  • How would you design a cloud flow to handle unexpected system errors, network timeouts, or API throttling gracefully?
  • Explain the importance of maintainability when building cloud flows and how this reduces technical debt.
  • His philosophy is "user-centered." How do you ensure the solutions you build solve a tangible problem for the people using them?
  • How do you explain technical concepts like API limitations to a non-technical audience?
  • What is your approach to User Acceptance Testing (UAT) for a Power App or automation?
  • Tell us about a time a critical Power Automate flow failed. Walk us through your troubleshooting and your communication process with stakeholders.
  • Describe your approach to creating documentation for a complex Power Platform solution.
  • How do you approach receiving constructive criticism on your work from a Lead Developer?
  • The Microsoft ecosystem changes rapidly. How do you adapt to new features, processes, and ways of working?
  • Describe your ideal team working environment and the role you typically play.
  • Tell us about a time you had a professional disagreement with a technical colleague. How did you resolve it?

Assessing the Business Strategist: Sharon Adkin

These questions assess your understanding of business value, strategic alignment, and your ability to function as a long-term asset. The goal is to prove you understand the "why" behind the technology and can deliver tangible return on investment for the public service mission.

  • Describe a time you identified an opportunity for automation. How did you build a business case focused on its value?
  • How do you quantify the potential ROI of an automation project in terms a Business Lead would value (e.g., time saved, error reduction, enabling officer visibility)?
  • Describe a situation where, after analysis, you recommended not to automate a process. What was your business-focused reasoning?
  • This role exists to fill a "skills gap." How do you see yourself delivering more value than an external contractor?
  • How can automation directly support the force's key performance objective of "Good use of resources"?
  • Walk us through how you would ensure an automation project delivers its promised benefits after deployment.
  • Describe a decision you had to make that balanced project deadlines against the need for additional hardening. How would you rationalize the decision to a business stakeholder?
  • How would you explain the business risk of using a single, generic user account for a desktop flow, beyond the technical reasons?
  • What is your motivation for a public sector role within the police, as opposed to a private sector role?
  • How do you see this role contributing to the Constabulary's Health and Wellbeing Strategy by reducing administrative burden?
  • How would you ensure your work consistently reflects the values of fairness, transparency, and public service?
  • What does the value of 'integrity' mean to you in a professional context?
  • Describe your understanding of the importance of public trust in policing. How can this role support it?
  • Beyond cybersecurity, what other risks (operational, reputational, ethical) should be considered when automating police processes?
  • When assessing a process, how do you evaluate the risk of losing valuable human oversight or judgement?
  • What are your long-term career aspirations, and how does this role fit into that vision?
  • Tell us about a situation where project requirements changed. How did you adapt and manage stakeholder expectations?
  • You are demonstrating a new automation and the team is resistant to change. What steps would you take to gain their buy-in?
  • What is your philosophy on providing support and documentation to empower users and build an internal capability?
  • How would you approach mentoring other team members to help build a sustainable, in-house automation capability?
  • If tasked with helping to establish a Power Platform 'Center of Excellence', what would be the key pillars of your strategy from a business perspective?
  • How do you ensure effective communication on a project involving multiple specialisms and non-technical stakeholders?
  • Describe a time you faced a significant setback in a project. What did you learn from the experience?
