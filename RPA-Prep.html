Strategic Interview Briefing: RPA Developer

Derbyshire Constabulary Panel: T. Litowczuk & S. Adkin

The Interview Dynamic

The interview is a synthesis of two priorities. **Taras Litowczuk (Technical Architect)** will assess your technical depth, architectural mindset, and ability to build secure, scalable solutions within the Microsoft Power Platform. **Sharon Adkin (Business Strategist)** will assess your understanding of business value, ROI, and your ability to function as a strategic asset who can fill the force's "skills gap" and deliver tangible results for the public service mission.

Questions from the Technical Architect

These questions test your technical depth, architectural mindset, and alignment with a user-centered, security-first development philosophy within the Microsoft ecosystem. The goal is to prove you are a professional developer, not just a coder.

  • Describe your most complex automation built using Power Automate, detailing your use of both cloud flows and desktop flows.
  • Explain how you have used Dataverse as a secure and scalable backend for a Power App or automation.
  • Tell us about a time you built a solution that deeply integrated Power Automate and SharePoint Online for document management.
  • Describe a complex problem you solved using PowerShell. How did it interact with other M356 or Azure services?
  • How do you decide when to use a Power Automate cloud flow versus a desktop flow? What are the architectural trade-offs?
  • What is your experience with Power Platform ALM and using solutions to move components between environments? Why is this critical?
  • How have you used or would you use AI Builder to create more intelligent automations?
  • What is your experience with version control systems like Azure DevOps or Git in the context of Power Platform development?
  • How would you approach debugging a complex cloud flow that is failing intermittently, using the tools available within the Power Platform?
  • How would you design an audit trail for a solution that updates records in Dataverse for legal defensibility?
  • Describe your experience implementing security roles in Dataverse to enforce role-based access control.
  • What is your preferred method for securing credentials and API keys for a Power Automate flow, referencing Azure Key Vault or similar?
  • Imagine you discover an existing cloud flow that stores a password in plain text. What are your immediate actions?
  • How do you ensure security is maintained throughout the entire Power Platform ALM?
  • Explain the role of Azure Active Directory and service principals in authentication for a solution using a custom connector.
  • Describe your experience deploying a Power Platform solution into a 24/7 live environment. How did you use solutions management to minimize disruption?
  • Tell us about a time a critical Power Automate flow failed. Walk us through your troubleshooting and communication process.
  • What is your strategy for testing a Power Automate desktop flow to ensure it is robust enough for a mission-critical environment?
  • How would you design a cloud flow to handle unexpected system errors, network timeouts, or API throttling gracefully?
  • Explain the importance of maintainability when building cloud flows and how this reduces technical debt.
  • What steps would you take before deploying an update to a Power App or flow in a live environment?

Questions from the Business Strategist

These questions assess your understanding of business value, strategic alignment, and your ability to function as a long-term asset. The goal is to prove you understand the "why" behind the technology and can deliver a tangible return on investment.

  • Describe a time you identified an opportunity for automation. How did you build a business case focused on its value?
  • How do you quantify the potential ROI of an automation project in terms a Business Lead would value (e.g., time saved, error reduction, enabling officer visibility)?
  • Describe a situation where, after analysis, you recommended not to automate a process. What was your business-focused reasoning?
  • This role exists to fill a "skills gap." How do you see yourself delivering more value than an external contractor?
  • How can automation directly support the force's key performance objective of "Good use of resources"?
  • Walk us through how you would ensure an automation project delivers its promised benefits after deployment.
  • Taras Litowczuk's philosophy is "user-centered." How do you ensure the solutions you build solve a tangible problem for the people using them?
  • How do you explain technical limitations to a non-technical manager who needs a solution urgently?
  • Tell us about a situation where project requirements changed. How did you adapt and manage stakeholder expectations?
  • What is your approach to User Acceptance Testing to ensure the business is happy with the final product?
  • You are demonstrating a new automation and the team is resistant to change. What steps would you take to gain their buy-in?
  • What is your philosophy on providing support and documentation to empower users and build an internal capability?
  • What is your motivation for a public sector role within the police, as opposed to a private sector role?
  • How do you see this role contributing to the Constabulary's Health and Wellbeing Strategy by reducing administrative burden?
  • How would you approach mentoring other team members to help build a sustainable, in-house automation capability?
  • If tasked with helping to establish a Power Platform 'Center of Excellence', what would be the key pillars of your strategy from a business perspective?
  • How do you ensure your work consistently reflects the values of fairness, transparency, and public service?

Synthesis & Scenario Questions

These questions test your ability to synthesize the technical and business perspectives. They assess your judgement and ability to navigate the inherent tensions between Mr. Litowczuk's technical mandate and Ms. Adkin's business objectives.

  • Sharon Adkin is pushing for a rapid deployment to meet an urgent need, but you believe Taras Litowczuk's standards for security testing have not been met. Describe the steps you would take.
  • A user requests a flow that emails them a report with sensitive data. How would you explain to them, and potentially to Ms. Adkin, why this is a risk, while also proposing an alternative solution that meets their underlying business need?
  • A business team wants to use a single, generic user account for a desktop flow for simplicity. How would you explain the technical risks to Mr. Litowczuk and the business risks to Ms. Adkin?
  • Describe a decision you had to make that balanced project deadlines against the need for additional security hardening. How did you rationalize the decision to both technical and business stakeholders?
  • A user story requires caching data in a SharePoint list for faster Power App performance, but security protocols forbid it. How do you resolve this technical and procedural conflict, satisfying both sides?
  • Beyond cybersecurity threats, what other risks (operational, reputational, ethical) should be considered when automating processes within a police force?
  • Consider automating a process for redacting sensitive information from documents. What are the primary risks, and how could a Power Platform solution be designed to mitigate them?
  • How could the automation of data entry potentially introduce unforeseen biases into a dataset? How might you guard against this from both a technical and business process perspective?
  • Describe how over-reliance on a single, complex cloud flow could create a new single point of failure. How would you explain this risk to Ms. Adkin?
  • When assessing a process for automation, how do you evaluate the risk of losing the valuable human oversight that was part of the manual process?

Organizational Alignment & Values

This final section assesses your alignment with the specific values and mission of Derbyshire Constabulary. It explores your professional motivations, your approach to teamwork, and your commitment to personal development.

  • What does the value of 'integrity' mean to you in a professional context?
  • Describe your ideal team working environment. What role do you typically play in a team?
  • Tell us about a time you had a professional disagreement with a colleague. How did you resolve it?
  • How do you ensure effective communication on a project that involves multiple specialisms like data science and cloud engineering?
  • Provide an example of a time when you contributed to the success of a team, even when it was outside of your direct responsibilities.
  • Describe a time you faced a significant setback or failure in a project. What did you learn from the experience?
  • How do you approach receiving constructive criticism or feedback on your work from a Lead Developer?
  • What area of the Power Platform or Azure are you currently seeking to develop, and what steps are you taking to do so?
  • The Microsoft ecosystem changes rapidly. How do you adapt to new features, processes, and ways of working?
  • What are your long-term career aspirations, and how does this role at Derbyshire Constabulary fit into that vision?
